Ultimate Jumper

Frontend

If you want to play I invite you to click on the image.

I developed this game during my first year of BUT, as part of a school project. Entirely developed in javascript, with a free sprite sheet found on the internet, the map coming from the platform game Ultimate Chicken Horse and modified by myself to add the platforms.

The notable points are the handling of collisions, the animation of the sprite sheet, the creation of gravity, the implementation of the jump and the detection of the success of the player to change the map.

The faults of this game are the lack of a welcome menu and a point counter, the absence of a defeat detection which forces the user to reload the page, the non-playability on mobile and obviously non-original graphics and not free of rights which create copyright problems if it was not a school project.

Sweet Demolition

Frontend

If you want to play I invite you to click on the image. The game is on the website Replit, you just have to create a account and run the code.

This game was for me my very first development during my last year of high school. It is also in a school setting that I realized it but with a friend who took care of the graphics. It is a development from scratch without a separate library for the graphics part.

The technical part of the game works and the graphics part is finished, unfortunately the linking of the two generates bugs that we have not had the time to correct.

As far as the technical part is concerned, the game takes place in a double-entry table, a matrix, with a scan after each turn of the players, which detects the presence or not of paterns of pawns defined to trigger their destruction then a fall of the pawns above and a random regeneration of the pawns.

As for the faults, we find the absence of graphics, no victory or defeat system or even an objective to make you want to play and finally the presence of a single square card and no other game mechanics that would add depth to the gameplay.
